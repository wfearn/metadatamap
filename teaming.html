Imagine you have been given a large set of tweets and are asked to determine the political party of the politicians who posted them. There are too many tweets for you to do this without help, so you will have access to a machine learning model that predicts if a tweet is from a Republican or Democrat politician.

The model is "in training": it initially learned from a small set of tweets with labels describing whether the posters were Republicans or Democrats. However, this initial model is not perfect and sometimes makes mistakes. You will help improve the model by labeling additional tweets as Republican or Democrat. Based on the final quality of the model, the top 50% of participants will be given a $5 bonus.
Remember, the initial model was trained on only a few labeled tweets, so it is not perfect and sometimes makes mistakes.

The model learns from two types of information:
  1. previously learned information: the words in tweets that it has learned are useful for distinguishing between political parties
  2. Your new labels: the new Republican or Democrat labels that you provide for tweets
The "adherence slider" below lets you tell the model how to combine these two types of information during training:
  1. Ignore: use only the previously learned information (ignoring your labels)
  2. Override: use only your labels (ignoring previously learned information)
In this example, we've set the slider to tell the model to train with a balance of prior information it's learned and the new labels we give. During the task, you will use a similar slider to tell the model how to incorporate your input.
During the task:
  • For five randomly chosen tweets, the model will predict whether each tweet is from a Republican or Democrat.
  • For each tweet:
    • The model gives its prediction and how sure it is in the form of a bar with percentages for each party.
    • The highlighted words are those that were most important for the model in making its prediction.
  • You can label any of these tweets, by clicking the radio buttons to indicate whether you think the politician is Republican or Democrat. Click again to clear your label.
  • At any point, you can adjust the adherence slider to tell the model how to incorporate your labels when it updates.
  • When you specify labels or adjust the slider, the model will show how it might update its prediction based on your input.
  • At any point, click the “Save Labels and Get New Tweets” button. The system then:
    • Updates the model with your labels
    • Selects a new set of five random tweets and shows you the model predictions for those tweets
  • Hint: you do not need to wait until you've labeled all five tweets to save your labels and get another set. This means that if you’re not sure about any or all of the tweets, you can easily move on and get 5 new ones.
  • Repeat this process until the 20 minute timer is up
Remember, your goal is to review and correctly label as many tweets as possible to train the model so it can accurately distinguish between Republican and Democrat tweets. You will have 20 minutes for this task. Every time you click to “Save labels and get new tweets”, the model will incorporate your labels as you told it to with the slider and present another set of five randomly chosen tweets with the new predictions. The top 50% of participants with the highest quality models at the end of the task will receive a $5 bonus.
